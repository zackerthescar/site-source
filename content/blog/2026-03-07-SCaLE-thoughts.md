# The Framework Desktop fits better into their lineup than you think

People keep bemoaning the Framework Desktop for not having any upgradable
RAM, and I was among them for the longest time. That's until I held it in
my hand for the first time at Framework's booth.

This thing is ungodly tiny. This thing isn't meant to be your desktop. It's
an appliance for efficient high-end crunchy compute (think AI, remote video
editing, etc) at your fingertips (with either a Tailscale-style VPN or just
to carry with you in your backpack). 

I'm still waiting for prices to trend downwards a little before I commit to
one, but it's very much something I'd like to get as the flagship for the
SF rendition of the homelab - bigger, badder, meaner, and (mildly) warmer.

# It's suprisingly easy to filter out AI if you're not interested

While doing my masters I had to do a semester's worth of symposium where we
just heard presentations from researchers. That really did put a bad taste
in me w.r.t AI research. That changed at the Noisebridge infra meetup when
we read the vLLM paper, but I was definitely more interested in attending
low-level stuff. There's something to be said about how many accepted talks
are about AI, but it's surprisingly easy to filter it out. PL and low-level
are still getting the attention they deserve, and they're not going away
anytime soon. I won't use buzzwords like 'force multiplier' or 'value add',
but know that (at least I firmly believe) domain knowledge is still important.

# RISC-V really needs to get its sh\*\* together

(thank Owen for this one) RISC-V community has offered Fyra Labs use of their
GitHub runners to build RISC-V Terra packages. Great! Except that their _one_
runner on offer has RVA23. It's just... kinda mandatory for _any_ 
general-purpose architecture to have some form of SIMD/Vectors, and RVA23 is
the first RISC-V standard for that. I remember hearing about RVA23 back in 2023.
Apparently silicon is only coming out now. This is fucked. General purpose
RISC-V silicon needs to get to market faster than this.
