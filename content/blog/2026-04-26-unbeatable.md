This is a ligher, shorter one. It's honestly nice to write something shorter
for once, as I'm trying to write more. The next one I think is one of my 
heaviest, but this one is properly light.

To inaugurate my new computer in SF, I picked up a game and finished the story.
It's been a while since I've beaten a game. It has a softlock on the latest
Proton. The beatmaps get really weird at some points. The 2D-characters-on-3D
was a really neat idea but needs a *lot* more baking time. The story is all
over the place. The beatmap regularly desyncs with the audio.

It's still my favorite game of all the games I've played in the last two years
or so.

UNBEATABLE follows Beat as she's thrust into an unknown world where music clearly
exists but is illegal. Beat meets Quaver and hijinks ensue as they both have to 
navigate not only the physical but also the emotional. 

Beat, I think, is one of the most relatable characters I've encountered in any
story, but only if you were/are a specific character in life. I just so happen
to be.

It's not for everyone, but if this game is for you, you'll know it. I think 
you'll have to play it for yourself to find out. 

I also think there's something to be said about messy, imperfect stories and
how they resonate with me. Not everything has to have a perfect hero's journey.
UNBEATABLE kind of does, but it gets really messy at the end. Evangelion
definitely doesn't, and you can ask people around me on how that story has
touched me. Humans, as it turns out, are really messy, and if you try to make 
a clean story, you end up losing the humanity. UNBEATABLE has tons of humanity
in it. I think that's why I like it so much.

Oh and the music *bops* so much. I know the devs took 7 years to make this game,
I just think a good chunk of why this game bops so much is because they spent so
much effort into the music and the end result shows (in a really good way).
